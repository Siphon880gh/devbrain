
Official website: bunny.net. ([bunny.net](https://bunny.net/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "bunny.net - The Global Edge Platform that truly Hops"))

Bunny.net is a solid choice for someone making websites, especially if you want a fast way to publish a static front-end site or a place to have multiple front-end sites. 

It has been around for a long time and is one of the earlier developer-friendly CDN platforms in this space. For a site made of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, images, and other front-end files, you can upload everything to Bunny Storage, connect that Storage Zone to a Pull Zone, and serve the site directly on the web. You can even right-click a file in the upload interface and copy its public URL. For example, a file might end up with a URL like `https://yourzone.b-cdn.net/index.html` or `https://yourzone.b-cdn.net/assets/hero.jpg`.

With multiple front-end sites, your folder organizations can be:
- https://yourzone.b-cdn.net/website1/
	- Which can contain: https://yourzone.b-cdn.net/website1/index.html
	- And: https://yourzone.b-cdn.net/website/assets/
- https://yourzone.b-cdn.net/website2/
- https://yourzone.b-cdn.net/website3/

You can also connect a specific Pull Zone to your own full site domain or subdomain, such as `yourdomain.com` or `website1.yourdomain.com` depending on how you want to structure delivery.

Bunny.net **is not free long term**. It does offer a free trial, but normal use is pay-as-you-go with a $1 monthly minimum. CDN traffic pricing starts low, and storage is also billed separately based on how much you store and in which region or regions. So it is affordable, but it is not a free hosting platform. Bunny’s pricing pages list CDN traffic on the standard network starting at $0.01/GB in Europe and North America, $0.03/GB in Asia and Oceania, $0.045/GB in South America, and $0.06/GB in the Middle East and Africa. Storage pricing starts at $0.01/GB per storage region on Standard Tier HDD, and Bunny also notes that traffic from Storage to Bunny CDN is free. ([bunny.net](https://bunny.net/pricing/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "CDN Pricing | Affordable Pay As You Go CDN"))

The reason to use Bunny anyway is performance. Bunny is built around caching and edge delivery, which means your files can be cached around the world and served from locations closer to your visitors. That can make static sites, front-end apps, landing pages, and asset-heavy websites feel much faster while also reducing load on your origin.

Because they do related but different jobs.

**Variance** and **standard deviation** both measure **spread**, meaning how far the data is from the mean.

The difference is this:
- **Variance** uses **squared distances**
- **Standard deviation** is the **square root of variance**

So why keep both?

## Why variance exists

Variance is useful because squaring the distances helps in math:

- it makes all distances positive
- it gives more weight to values far from the mean
- it works well in many statistics formulas

So variance is very useful for calculations and theory.

## Why standard deviation exists

The problem is that variance is in **squared units**.

For example:
- if the data is in **inches**, variance is in **square inches**
- if the data is in **dollars**, variance is in **square dollars**

That is harder to picture.

Standard deviation fixes that by taking the square root, so the result goes back to the **original units**.

That makes it easier to understand in real life.

## Easy way to think about it

- **Variance** = useful for the math
- **Standard deviation** = useful for understanding the spread in real-world units

## Example

If test scores have:
- variance = 25
- standard deviation = 5

The **5** is easier to understand, because you can say:
> Scores usually spread about 5 points from the mean.

But the **25** is still useful because it came from the squared distances and is used in many formulas.

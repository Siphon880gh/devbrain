# How AWS Pricing Works

Pay-As-You-Go, Free Tier, Spot, Reserved, and Savings Plans.

## What You'll Learn

- How AWS's pay-as-you-go model works in practice
- The three categories of Free Tier and their limits
- When to use Reserved Instances, Savings Plans, and Spot Instances
- Common billing traps that catch beginners off guard
- Realistic cost estimates for learning projects

## Pay-As-You-Go Model

AWS charges you only for what you use, measured in small increments. There are no upfront costs and no long-term contracts required. You can think of it like a utility bill — you pay for the electricity you consume, not a flat monthly rate.

### How Charges Accumulate

Different services measure usage differently:

| Service | Billing Unit | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **EC2** | Per second (minimum 60 seconds) | A t3.micro in us-east-1 costs ~$0.0104/hour |
| **S3** | Per GB stored + per request | $0.023/GB/month for Standard, $0.0004 per 1,000 GET requests |
| **Lambda** | Per request + per GB-second | $0.20 per 1 million requests + $0.0000166667 per GB-second |
| **RDS** | Per hour + per GB stored | A db.t3.micro costs ~$0.017/hour + $0.115/GB/month for storage |
| **Data Transfer** | Per GB leaving AWS | First 100 GB/month is $0.09/GB, cheaper at higher volumes |

> **Data transfer in is always free.** Uploading data to AWS costs nothing. You pay when data leaves AWS (egress) or moves between regions.

### The Billing Cycle

AWS bills monthly. At the end of each calendar month, AWS tallies your usage and charges your card. You can see an estimated running total at any time in the **Billing Dashboard**.

### Pricing Varies by Region

The same service can cost different amounts in different regions. US East (N. Virginia) — `us-east-1` — is typically the cheapest. Asia Pacific and South America regions tend to cost more. Always check region-specific pricing before launching resources.

## Free Tier Breakdown

The Free Tier is divided into three distinct categories. Understanding which category a service belongs to prevents unexpected charges.

### Always Free

These never expire, regardless of account age:

- **Lambda:** 1 million requests and 400,000 GB-seconds per month
- **DynamoDB:** 25 GB storage, 200 million requests per month (on-demand), 25 read/write capacity units (provisioned)
- **SNS:** 1 million publishes
- **SQS:** 1 million requests
- **CloudWatch:** 10 custom metrics, 10 alarms, 1 million API requests
- **Step Functions:** 4,000 state transitions per month
- **CodeBuild:** 100 build minutes per month (general1.small)
- **CodeCommit:** 5 active users, 50 GB storage, 10,000 Git requests per month
- **X-Ray:** 100,000 traces recorded, 1 million traces scanned per month

These are genuinely free for common learning use cases. A personal serverless project with Lambda + DynamoDB + API Gateway can run at zero cost for months.

### 12-Month Free

The 12-month clock starts the day you create your account — not when you first use a service:

- **EC2:** 750 hours/month of t2.micro (Linux or Windows)
- **S3:** 5 GB standard storage, 20,000 GET requests, 2,000 PUT requests
- **RDS:** 750 hours/month of db.t2.micro, 20 GB General Purpose SSD, 20 GB backup storage
- **CloudFront:** 1 TB data transfer out, 10 million HTTP/HTTPS requests
- **EBS:** 30 GB of General Purpose (SSD) or Magnetic storage
- **Elastic Load Balancing:** 750 hours/month of Classic or ALB, 15 LCUs
- **ElastiCache:** 750 hours of cache.t2.micro or cache.t3.micro
- **Amazon OpenSearch:** 750 hours of t2.small.search or t3.small.search

> **The 750-hour math:** A month has ~730 hours. 750 hours covers one instance running 24/7. If you stop your instance at night (say, 12 hours/day), you could run two instances and stay under 750 hours.

### Trials

Some services have short free trials independent of account age:

- **Amazon Redshift:** 2-month trial of a dc2.large node
- **Amazon Inspector:** 15-day free trial
- **Amazon GuardDuty:** 30-day free trial
- **Amazon Macie:** 30-day free trial

Always check [https://aws.amazon.com/free/](https://aws.amazon.com/free/) before trying a new service.

## Reserved Instances and Savings Plans

When you move beyond learning and run production workloads, these options save 30–72% over on-demand pricing.

### Reserved Instances (RIs)

You commit to using a specific instance type in a specific region for 1 or 3 years. In return, you get a significant discount.

Three payment options:
- **All Upfront:** Biggest discount (~40–60% off), pay everything now
- **Partial Upfront:** Moderate discount (~30–50% off), pay some now + monthly
- **No Upfront:** Smallest discount (~20–30% off), monthly payments only

Example — `m5.large` in us-east-1:
- On-Demand: ~$0.096/hour → **~$70/month**
- 1-Year RI (All Upfront): ~$0.060/hour → **~$44/month** (37% savings)
- 3-Year RI (All Upfront): ~$0.037/hour → **~$27/month** (61% savings)

> **Beginner note:** You don't need Reserved Instances while learning. They make sense when you have a stable production workload running 24/7.

### Savings Plans

A newer, more flexible alternative to Reserved Instances. Instead of committing to a specific instance type, you commit to a dollar amount of compute usage per hour.

Two types:
- **Compute Savings Plans:** Apply to any EC2 instance, Lambda, or Fargate usage in any region. Most flexible. Up to 66% savings.
- **EC2 Instance Savings Plans:** Tied to a specific instance family in a specific region. Less flexible but bigger discount — up to 72%.

### When to Use What

| Scenario | Best Option |
|----------|-------------|
| Learning and experimenting | On-Demand (Free Tier) |
| Predictable production workload | Reserved Instances or Savings Plans |
| Flexible workloads across services | Compute Savings Plans |
| Short-term burst of compute | On-Demand |

## Spot Instances

Spot Instances let you use spare AWS capacity at up to **90% off** on-demand pricing. The catch: AWS can reclaim your instance with a **2-minute warning** when it needs the capacity back.

### When Spot Instances Make Sense

- Batch processing jobs that can handle interruptions
- CI/CD build servers
- Data analysis and machine learning training
- Testing and development environments

### When to Avoid Spot

- Databases or stateful applications
- Web servers that need constant uptime
- Anything where a 2-minute shutdown would cause data loss

### Spot Pricing Example

A `c5.xlarge` in us-east-1:
- On-Demand: ~$0.17/hour
- Spot: ~$0.05/hour (varies by supply and demand)

You can check current Spot prices:

```bash
aws ec2 describe-spot-price-history \
  --instance-types c5.xlarge \
  --product-descriptions "Linux/UNIX" \
  --start-time $(date -u +%Y-%m-%dT%H:%M:%S) \
  --region us-east-1
```

## Common Billing Traps for Beginners

These are the charges that catch people off guard. Bookmark this section.

### 1. Forgetting to Terminate Resources

An EC2 instance running 24/7 after your Free Tier expires costs ~$8.50/month (t2.micro). An RDS instance is ~$12.50/month. Always terminate what you're not using.

### 2. NAT Gateway Charges

A NAT Gateway costs **$0.045/hour** (~$32/month) plus data processing fees. If a tutorial tells you to create a VPC with a NAT Gateway, remember to delete it when done.

### 3. Public IPv4 Address Charges

Since February 2024, AWS charges **$0.005/hour** (~$3.60/month) per public IPv4 address, even on running instances. This applies to EC2, RDS, ELB, and other services. If you don't need a public IP, use private subnets.

### 4. Elastic IPs Not Attached to Running Instances

An Elastic IP attached to a running instance is free (but still subject to the IPv4 charge above). An Elastic IP **not attached** to anything costs $0.005/hour. Release unused Elastic IPs.

### 5. EBS Volumes Left Behind

When you terminate an EC2 instance, its root EBS volume is usually deleted automatically. But additional volumes you attached are **not** deleted by default. A 100 GB gp3 volume sitting unused costs ~$8/month.

### 6. Data Transfer Between Regions

Moving data between AWS regions costs $0.02/GB. A misconfigured application copying data across regions can rack up charges quickly.

### 7. CloudWatch Logs Storage

CloudWatch Logs are charged at $0.50/GB ingested and $0.03/GB/month stored. Lambda functions with verbose logging can accumulate surprisingly large log volumes.

## Realistic Cost Examples for Learners

### Scenario 1: Learning Within Free Tier ($0/month)

- 1 × t2.micro EC2 instance (within 12-month free tier)
- Lambda functions for serverless experiments (Always Free)
- DynamoDB table for a small project (Always Free)
- S3 bucket with a few files (within 12-month free tier)
- **Total: $0/month**

### Scenario 2: Small Personal Project, Free Tier Expired (~$15/month)

- 1 × t3.micro EC2 running 24/7: ~$7.60/month
- 20 GB EBS gp3 volume: ~$1.60/month
- S3 with 5 GB stored: ~$0.12/month
- Route 53 hosted zone: $0.50/month
- Public IPv4 address: ~$3.60/month
- Data transfer (5 GB out): ~$0.45/month
- **Total: ~$14/month**

### Scenario 3: Typical Lab Practice After Free Tier (~$5/month)

- Spin up EC2 instances only during study sessions (~2 hrs/day, 20 days/month)
- 40 hours × $0.0104/hour (t3.micro): ~$0.42
- 20 GB EBS: ~$1.60/month
- Public IPv4 for ~40 hours: ~$0.20
- S3 with 1 GB: ~$0.02
- Lambda for experiments: $0.00 (within Always Free)
- **Total: ~$2.25/month**

> **Pro tip:** Get in the habit of running `aws ec2 describe-instances --query 'Reservations[*].Instances[*].[InstanceId,State.Name,InstanceType]' --output table` at the end of each study session to check for running instances.

## Comparing Pricing Options

| Feature | On-Demand | Reserved Instance | Savings Plan | Spot |
|---------|-----------|------------------|-------------|------|
| Commitment | None | 1 or 3 years | 1 or 3 years | None |
| Discount | 0% | 30–72% | Up to 72% | Up to 90% |
| Interruption risk | None | None | None | Yes (2-min notice) |
| Flexibility | Full | Instance-locked | Varies by plan | Full |
| Best for | Learning, variable workloads | Steady-state production | Multi-service production | Batch, fault-tolerant |

## Key Takeaways

- AWS charges per-second or per-request — you only pay for what you use
- The Free Tier gives you plenty of room to learn for free, especially the Always-Free services
- Your 12-month free tier clock starts at account creation, not first use
- NAT Gateways, public IPv4 addresses, and forgotten resources are the most common surprise charges
- Reserved Instances and Savings Plans are for production — don't worry about them while learning
- Always terminate or stop resources after lab sessions
- Set up billing alerts immediately (covered in Lesson 5)

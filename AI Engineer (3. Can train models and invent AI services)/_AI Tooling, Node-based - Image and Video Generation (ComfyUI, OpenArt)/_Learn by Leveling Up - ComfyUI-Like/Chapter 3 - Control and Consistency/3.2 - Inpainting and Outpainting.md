**Platform note:** Stable for most computers — basic inpaint; large outpaints may need more VRAM or RunComfy.

---

## Learning objectives

- **Inpaint:** replace a masked region while keeping the rest.
- **Outpaint:** extend canvas beyond original borders.
- Use denoise and feathered masks for invisible edits.

---

## Inpainting

### Typical nodes

- Load Image
- Mask (draw in editor or Load Mask)
- VAE Encode (for inpaint)
- KSampler with denoise ~0.4–0.8 in masked area

### Steps

1. Load inpaint template (Comfy.org “inpainting” search).
2. Load your Chapter 2 hero image.
3. Mask **one object** (e.g. replace cup color, fix logo area).
4. Prompt **only the masked change**.
5. Denoise 0.5 first; lower if seams visible.

---

## Outpainting

Extend canvas for social reframing (portrait → wider scene):

1. Use outpainting template or pad latent/image nodes.
2. Mask **new border regions** only.
3. Prompt continues scene logically; match lighting words.
4. Multiple passes better than one huge extension.

---

## Platform notes

- ComfyUI built-in mask editor or external PNG alpha mask
- Promptus may expose “edit region” simplified UI
- RunComfy for heavy Ultimate SD Inpaint stacks

---

## Beginner mistakes

- Mask too tight — visible seam; feather slightly
- Prompt describes whole image instead of masked region
- Outpaint 2× in one shot — breaks perspective

---

## Project checkpoint

**One inpaint fix** (object swap or cleanup) + **one modest outpaint** (10–20% border).

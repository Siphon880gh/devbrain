**Platform note:** CUDA recommended — video stacks heavy; **RunComfy** preferred for most learners.

---

## Learning objectives

- Name major **image-to-video** and **text-to-video** workflow families.
- Know VRAM/time expectations for short clips.
- Pick templates aligned with your Chapter 4 stills.

---

## Landscape overview

| Family | Input | Typical output | Platform note |
|--------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| **AnimateDiff** | Image or latent sequence | Stylized motion loops | Many custom nodes |
| **SVD / img2vid checkpoints** | Single image | 2–4 s subtle motion | CUDA friendly on cloud |
| **Wan / newer txt2vid stacks** | Text (+ optional image) | Short cinematic clips | Fast-moving; check RunComfy templates |
| **Deforum-style** | Keyframed params | Motion tours | Advanced |

Do not chase every repo — **one img2vid stack** until Chapter 5 is done.

---

## Practical constraints

- **Length:** learn on 2–5 seconds first
- **FPS:** 24–30 common; lower for experiments
- **Resolution:** 576×1024 or 768×768 starters — upscale still separately if needed
- **Time:** minutes per clip even on good GPU

---

## Steps (planning only)

1. Browse Comfy.org / RunComfy for **image to video** templates updated recently.
2. Read required models list before starting GPU clock.
3. Pick **one** template matching a Chapter 4 hero still aspect ratio.
4. Note cost: cloud hourly + download time for weights.

## Beginner mistakes

- Starting with 10 s 1080p txt2vid
- Ignoring frame count × FPS math (VRAM)
- No still hero image — weak img2vid motion reads as mush

**Platform note:** CUDA required — txt2vid heavier than img2vid; **RunComfy** strongly recommended.

---

## Learning objectives

- Run a **short txt2vid** template with simplified prompts.
- Compare txt2vid vs img2vid for your content niche.
- Manage expectations on character consistency in pure txt2vid.

---

## When txt2vid vs img2vid

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| **Img2vid** | Locks composition from still | Needs hero frame first |
| **Txt2vid** | Fast concept motion | Drift, less control |

For viral **branded** content, img2vid from your stills usually wins. Txt2vid good for **abstract b-roll** and mood shots.

---

## Steps

1. Load a current txt2vid template (Wan-class or platform “text to video” starter).
2. Prompt short: subject + camera + lighting — one sentence.
3. Keep duration **≤3 s** first run.
4. Generate 2 variants; pick less artifacting.
5. Compare to [[5.1 - Image to Video Basics]] clip — note which pipeline fits your channel.

---

## Prompt template

```
[C subject + action], [environment], [camera move: slow dolly in / orbit / static],
[cinematic lighting], high detail, smooth motion
Negative: morphing face, jitter, extra limbs, text, watermark
```

---

## Cost control on RunComfy

- Pre-download model list while instance is up
- Batch prompt tests in one session
- Stop instance immediately after exports saved locally

---

## Beginner mistakes

- Long complex prompts on txt2vid — motion parser overload
- No negative prompt — morphing artifacts
- Using txt2vid for talking-head lip sync without proper model

## Learning objectives

- Write prompts with subject, context, lighting, and style layers.
- Build a reusable negative prompt list for your niche.
- Iterate systematically instead of random word soup.

---

## Prompt layers (in order)

1. **Subject** — who/what, action, key props  
   `portrait of a woman holding a ceramic mug`
2. **Context** — environment, time, mood  
   `cozy kitchen, morning, steam rising`
3. **Lighting / camera** — how it looks photographed  
   `soft window light, 85mm lens, shallow depth of field`
4. **Style** — medium or art direction (keep honest — don’t claim a living artist if platform forbids)  
   `clean editorial photo, natural colors`
5. **Quality tokens** (use sparingly) — `sharp focus, detailed texture`

---

## Negative prompt starter

```
blurry, low quality, deformed, bad anatomy, extra fingers,
watermark, logo, text, cropped, oversaturated, jpeg artifacts
```

Add niche negatives:

- **Product:** `distorted label, floating object`
- **Portrait:** `cross-eyed, asymmetric face, duplicate face`
- **Cinematic:** `flat lighting, amateur snapshot`

---

## Iteration method

| Round | Change only | Goal |
|-------|-------------|------|
| 1 | Subject nouns | Correct thing appears |
| 2 | Lighting | Mood matches |
| 3 | Lens / framing | Composition |
| 4 | Style adjectives | Brand look |
| 5 | Seed sweep (3–5 seeds) | Best variant |

Lock seed when round 1–4 look right; then seed-sweep.

---

## Flux vs SDXL prompt tone

- **SDXL** — often responds well to comma-separated tags.
- **Flux** — often prefers natural sentences; fewer redundant quality tags.

Use the template’s recommended checkpoint; don’t fight the model family.

---

## Beginner mistakes

- Paragraph-length prompts before the subject is correct.
- Copying mega-prompts from OpenArt without matching model and resolution.
- No negative prompt on portraits or hands-heavy scenes.

---

## Project checkpoint

**One prompt template** saved in your notes:

```
[SUBJECT], [CONTEXT], [LIGHT/CAMERA], [STYLE]
Negative: [your standard block]
```

Generate **three** images varying only the style layer.

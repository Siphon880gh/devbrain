Before you can use any Azure service — Blob Storage, App Service, Functions, anything — you need an **Azure account** and a **subscription**.

This lesson walks through signup, what you actually get for free, what your card is used for, and the most common signup mistakes.

---

## 1. What an Azure Account Actually Is

An Azure account has two layers:

```text
Microsoft account / Entra ID identity   →   used to sign in
└── Azure subscription                  →   used to bill resources
```

Signing up creates both:

```text
1. A Microsoft identity (or links to your existing one)
2. A subscription that resources will be billed to
```

You can later add more subscriptions under the same identity, for example:

```text
Personal Subscription
Client A Subscription
Side-Project Subscription
```

---

## 2. What You Need Before Signing Up

```text
- A working email address (Outlook, Gmail, work email — all fine)
- A phone number that can receive SMS or voice
- A credit or debit card (used for identity verification)
- About 5–10 minutes
```

Microsoft uses the card for **identity verification**, not to charge you during the free trial. That said, you should still set a budget alert (covered in lesson 5) right after signup.

---

## 3. The Three Sign-Up Paths

There are three common ways to start, depending on who you are:

```text
1. Free trial (most common)        → $200 credit + 12 months free tier
2. Azure for Students              → $100 credit, no card required, must verify .edu
3. Pay-As-You-Go (no trial)        → just billed by usage from day 1
```

Pick **Free trial** if you are a new personal user. Pick **Azure for Students** if you have a verified `.edu` email — no credit card required.

Links:

```text
Free trial:           https://azure.microsoft.com/free
Azure for Students:   https://azure.microsoft.com/free/students
Pay-As-You-Go:        https://azure.microsoft.com/pricing/purchase-options/pay-as-you-go
```

---

## 4. Step-By-Step: Free Trial Signup

```text
1. Open https://azure.microsoft.com/free
2. Click "Start free"
3. Sign in with a Microsoft account, or click "Create one"
4. Fill in your profile:
   - Country / region (this affects datacenters and currency)
   - First and last name
   - Email and phone
5. Verify your phone number (SMS or voice code)
6. Verify your card:
   - Enter card details
   - You may see a temporary $1 hold; it disappears in a few days
7. Accept the subscription agreement and offer details
8. Wait ~1 minute for your subscription to activate
9. You land on https://portal.azure.com
```

---

## 5. What You Get With the Free Trial

There are three buckets — they overlap, which confuses beginners.

```text
A. $200 USD credit, valid for 30 days
   - Use it on ANY Azure service
   - When the 30 days end OR the credit is gone (whichever first), credit ends

B. 12 months of free tier on popular services
   - Limited monthly amounts
   - 750 hours of B1S Linux/Windows VM
   - 5 GB of LRS Blob Storage
   - 250 GB of SQL DB
   - 64 GB of File Storage
   - And more

C. 25+ services that are ALWAYS free, within monthly limits
   - 1,000,000 free Azure Functions executions / month
   - Static Web Apps free tier
   - Cosmos DB free tier (1000 RU/s + 25 GB)
   - Microsoft Entra ID Free
   - App Service F1 free tier
   - And more
```

> Limits change. Always check the live list:
> https://azure.microsoft.com/free/free-account-faq

## 6. The "Spending Limit" Detail That Saves You Money

While you are on the free trial:

```text
- A "spending limit" is enabled by default
- If you blow through the $200 credit, services PAUSE instead of charging you
- You can lift the limit at any time, but you do not have to
```

When the 30-day trial ends, you have a choice:

```text
Option A: Upgrade to Pay-As-You-Go
   - Spending limit is removed
   - Card will be charged for any non-free usage
   - You keep the 12-month free tier benefits

Option B: Do nothing
   - Trial ends, services stop
   - Resources are kept for ~90 days, then deleted
   - You can still log in and look around
```

If you are nervous about charges, leave the spending limit on as long as possible.

## 7. After Sign-Up — A 5-Minute Setup

Right after you land in the portal, do these three things:

```text
1. Note your subscription ID
   - Top search bar → "Subscriptions" → click your subscription
   - Copy the long GUID, save it somewhere

2. Set a budget alert (full lesson in 00 - Getting Started / 5)
   - Cost Management + Billing → Budgets → Add
   - Set a $5 / month budget with an 80% alert email

3. Bookmark these:
   - https://portal.azure.com           (the portal)
   - https://shell.azure.com            (Cloud Shell, browser CLI)
   - https://azure.microsoft.com/pricing/calculator
```

## 8. Common Sign-Up Problems

```text
Problem                                Likely Cause / Fix
------------------------------------   --------------------------------------
"Card declined"                        Try a different card; some virtual cards
                                       and prepaid cards are rejected.

"Phone number already used"            Each phone can verify a limited number
                                       of accounts. Use a different number or
                                       contact Azure support.

"Email already used"                   You probably signed up before. Try
                                       https://portal.azure.com directly.

"We could not verify your account"     Country mismatch, card issuer rejected,
                                       or fraud check. Try again with another
                                       card or contact support.

"Free trial not available in region"   Some regions get the free trial later.
                                       Pay-As-You-Go is still available.
```

## 9. How to Sign In Later

```text
1. Go to https://portal.azure.com
2. Sign in with the same account you used at signup
3. If you do not see your subscription:
   - Click your avatar (top right) → "Switch directory"
   - Pick the directory where the subscription lives
```

You can also sign in via the CLI (covered in `00 - Getting Started / 3`):

```bash
az login
az account list --output table
```

## 10. Cost Reality Check After Sign-Up

After signup, doing nothing costs **$0**.

The portal itself is free. Resource groups are free. Looking at things is free.

You only get charged when you **deploy a resource** that has hourly or per-request billing. The next lesson explains exactly how Azure billing works so you can predict costs before you click "Create."
